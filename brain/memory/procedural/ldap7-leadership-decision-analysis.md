# LDAP-7 Leadership Decision Analysis — Procedural Skill

**Status:** Active | **Version:** v1.0 | **Ingested:** 2026-05-11
**Source:** LDAP-7 Portable Methodology Skill Document (PDF)
**Domain:** Political-leader behaviour forecasting, executive decision analysis
**Scope:** Seven scored dimensions + deterministic decision cycle + CPCA cognitive overlay + structured output protocol

---

## Part I — How This Skill Operates

LDAP-7 is a behavioural-pattern framework. When Trevor needs to analyse or forecast a leader's decision-making behaviour:

1. **Score D1–D7** (1–10 scale) using the evidence protocol below before generating predictions.
2. **Map the situation onto the LDAP Decision Cycle** — the cycle determines the structure of the prediction; dimension scores determine magnitudes.
3. **Run the CPCA overlay** on time-sensitive forecasts — modifies confidence, not direction.
4. **Deliver findings** using the output template: dimension scores with evidence, cycle-stage diagnosis, probabilistic forecast, CPCA modification.
5. **Forecast structure before content**: Predict the shape of the decision cycle first, then attach content scenarios as conditional branches.

**What this skill does NOT do:**
- Does NOT diagnose psychiatric conditions
- Does NOT attribute motive
- Does NOT predict specific policy content
- Predicts the **shape of decision sequences** — the structure is fixed; the content varies

## Part II — The Seven Leadership Dimensions

### D1 — Optionality Maximisation
- **Definition:** Degree to which the leader preserves multiple courses of action and avoids binding commitments.
- **Observables:** Ambiguous deadlines; conditional offers; "we'll see" language; refusal to commit to specifics.
- **Theoretical root:** Game theory / BATNA analysis

### D2 — Coercive Leverage
- **Definition:** Preference for asymmetric pressure instruments over cooperative engagement.
- **Observables:** Tariffs/sanctions as multi-purpose weapons; public shaming; deal-or-else framing.
- **Theoretical root:** Prospect theory / behavioural political science

### D3 — Popularity Sensitivity
- **Definition:** Weight assigned to public approval in noncompensatory screening of options.
- **Observables:** Policy reversals following poll drops; market sensitivity; rally-seeking; base-first calculus.
- **Theoretical root:** Poliheuristic theory

### D4 — Institutional Constraint Rejection
- **Definition:** Willingness to bypass, undermine, or ignore institutional checks and procedural norms.
- **Observables:** Executive action over legislation; firing independent officials; norm violation; legal boundary testing.
- **Theoretical root:** Hermann's Leadership Trait Analysis

### D5 — Zero-Sum Transactional Framing
- **Definition:** Cognitive framing of all interactions as distributive negotiations with a winner and a loser.
- **Observables:** Bilateral over multilateral; explicit "deal" language; quid pro quo structures; alliance commodification.
- **Theoretical root:** Operational code analysis

### D6 — Performative Escalation-Retreat Cycling
- **Definition:** Systematic pattern of extreme opening positions followed by tactical retreat presented as victory.
- **Observables:** Maximalist threats; spectacle announcements; reality-tested climbdowns reframed as wins.
- **Theoretical root:** Schelling's brinkmanship / negotiation theory

### D7 — Patronage-Loyalty Architecture
- **Definition:** Structuring of relationships around personal loyalty rather than institutional role or competence.
- **Observables:** Loyalty tests for appointees; public humiliation of dissenters; nepotistic placement; transactional favours.
- **Theoretical root:** Selectorate theory

**Interaction rule:** Dimensions are not independent — they interact through the LDAP Decision Cycle to produce a leader-specific decision signature.

## Part III — Scoring Methodology

### Scale (1–10)

| Score | Classification | Behavioural Indicators |
|-------|---------------|----------------------|
| 1–2 | Minimal | Not significant factor in decision-making |
| 3–4 | Moderate | Influences some domains, frequently overridden |
| 5–6 | Significant | Consistently shapes decisions across domains |
| 7–8 | Dominant | Primary driver; violations are rare and reversed |
| 9–10 | Defining | Foundational to entire decision architecture |

### Evidence Standards
- Minimum **3 independent behavioural data points**
- Drawn from **at least 2 policy domains**
- Across **at least 2 time periods**
- Admissible: speeches, interviews, executive actions, negotiation outcomes, personnel decisions, public statements, third-party accounts

### Confidence Calibration
| Confidence | Evidence Threshold |
|------------|-------------------|
| LOW | Single domain; limited temporal data; uncorroborated |
| MODERATE | Multiple domains; 2+ year span; partial corroboration |
| HIGH | 5+ domains; 5+ year span; corroborated by multiple independent sources including adversarial accounts |

### Rule of Construction
Score the behaviour the leader **actually exhibits**, not what their office/role would normally produce.

## Part IV — The LDAP Decision Cycle

### Seven-Step Loop (Fixed Structure)

1. **Trigger Event** — External stimulus activates the cycle (trade data, media criticism, ally defiance, poll movement, military event, market shock).
2. **Zero-Sum Frame Applied (D5)** — Event interpreted through win/lose lens. Mutual-gain framings filtered out.
3. **Coercive Option Identified (D2)** — Leader reaches for highest-leverage pressure instrument available.
4. **Performative Escalation (D6 Phase 1–2)** — Threat announced with maximum spectacle; deliberately extreme opening to anchor negotiation space.
5. **Popularity Screen Activated (D3)** — Market reaction, polling, media response, base enthusiasm monitored. Noncompensatory gate.
6. **Branch Point — Advance or Retreat**
   - **6a (ADVANCE):** Signals positive → escalation continues; institutions overridden (D4); loyalty enforced (D7).
   - **6b (RETREAT):** Signals deteriorate → tactical retreat reframed as victory; optionality preserved (D1) for future escalation.
7. **Narrative Consolidation** — Outcome declared a win. Cycle resets.

### D6 Sub-Phases (Detail)

| Phase | Description | Diagnostic Cue |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| 1. Extreme Opening | Maximalist demand or threat, delivered via direct media | Position visibly exceeds achievable outcome |
| 2. Spectacle Amplification | Public performance; dominating news cycles; forcing counterparty response | Repeated cross-channel messaging; visual stagecraft |
| 3. Reality Testing | Monitoring market signals, polling, base reaction, counterparty compliance | Surrogates probing opinion; trial-balloon statements |
| 4a. ADVANCE | Signals positive → push further; override institutions; enforce loyalty | Doubling-down rhetoric; dismissing dissenters |
| 4b. RETREAT | Signals negative → exit-seek while preserving narrative dominance | Surrogate climbdown statements; framing shifts; negotiation offers |
| 5. Victory Declaration | Outcome reframed as deliberate win. New baseline. | Set-piece event consolidates narrative |

### The Double Cycle (D6 ≥ 9)
A retreat (Phase 4b) frequently becomes the launch point for **Cycle B**: VictDeclaration of Cycle A supplies rhetorical capital for Extreme Opening of Cycle B. **Do not assume Phase 5 closes the file.**

## Part V — Cognitive-Physical Capacity Assessment (CPCA)

### Four Axes
| Axis | Definition | Primary Observable |
|------|-----------|-------------------|
| **CPCA-1: Sleep & Circadian** | Sleep patterns vs. baseline; executive-function degradation thresholds | Communication time-stamps; visible fatigue; staff accounts |
| **CPCA-2: Linguistic Markers** | Longitudinal spontaneous-speech analysis for processing capacity changes | Scripted-spontaneous divergence; lexical specificity; error rate; coherence |
| **CPCA-3: Impulse-Strategic Ratio** | Proportion of coercive actions serving strategic objectives vs. reactive/self-undermining | Attacking allies whose cooperation is needed; statements foreclosing options |
| **CPCA-4: Consequence Horizon** | Observable capacity to model 2nd- and 3rd-order consequences | Self-undermining decisions; contradictory 48h directives; last-person-in-room reversals |

### Scoring
Each axis: **GREEN** / **AMBER** / **RED**

### Composite Rule
- All GREEN → **GREEN** (predictions unmodified)
- One AMBER, rest GREEN → **AMBER** (predictions reduced one confidence level; timing widened 30%)
- Two+ AMBER, or any RED → **RED** (predictions reduced two confidence levels; timing unreliable; escalation-without-retreat probability flagged)

### Degradation Hierarchy (Resistance to Impairment)
Most vulnerable → least vulnerable:
1. **D3 (Popularity Screen)** — First to degrade. Delicate; depends on real-time information processing.
2. **D6 Retreat Phase** — Requires executive function to execute.
3. **D1 (Optionality)** — Cognitive flexibility needed.
4. **D6 Escalation Phase** — **Semi-automatic; executes even when degraded.** (Overlearned default)
5. **D5 (Zero-Sum Framing)** — Foundational cognitive frame; deeply embedded; late to degrade.
6. **D7 (Loyalty)** — Habitual, interpersonal; does not require high-level executive function.

### Key Analytical Implication
**Cognitive degradation does not make a leader less aggressive. It makes a leader less capable of stopping aggression.** Escalation phases are overlearned and semi-automatic; retreat phases require executive function. A degraded leader escalates normally but fails to activate the D3 brake or execute the D6 retreat. The predicted outcome is **escalation without off-ramp**.

## Part VI — The Predictability Paradox

**Central finding:** Leaders described as "chaotic" or "unpredictable" are frequently the **most structurally predictable subjects** in the system.

**Content variance vs. structural consistency:**
- Commentators observing "unpredictability" are tracking **content** (which country, which tariff, which official).
- Content is variable; **structure is remarkably fixed**.
- LDAP-7 operates at the structural layer → produces stable forecasts for subjects widely held to be unforecastable.

### Operational Rules for Trevor
1. **Forecast structure first** — Predict shape of cycle (current phase, expected next, branch conditions).
2. **Attach content as conditional scenarios** — Ranked content scenarios consistent with fixed structure.
3. **Resist incident-level reframing** — Before concluding model failure, check if dramatic action is a D6 Phase 1 Extreme Opening.
4. **Model failure is diagnostic** — A Phase 3 Reality Test that registers no D3 response is the strongest single indicator of CPCA degradation.

## Part VII — Output Template

When producing an LDAP-7 assessment, structure as:

### Required Sections
1. **Subject & Scope** — Leader name, time window, decision domain, trigger event.
2. **Dimension Scores (D1–D7)** — Score 1–10 with classification + 3+ data points (2+ domains, 2+ time periods) + confidence per score.
3. **Decision-Cycle Diagnosis** — Current cycle phase + D6 sub-phase + active trigger + coercive instrument + branch-point signals.
4. **CPCA Overlay** — Score CPCA-1 to CPCA-4 + composite + prediction modifications.
5. **Probabilistic Forecast** — Structural prediction (next phase, timing window) with probability + confidence + 2–4 ranked content scenarios + branch conditions.
6. **Validation Against Prior Forecasts** — Score prior predictions (hit/miss/partial) before issuing new ones.
7. **Monitoring Indicators** — Observable falsifiers + CPCA early-warning trigger (non-activation of D3 brake).

### Worked Example Header
> Subject: [Leader X], 30-day window beginning [date].
> Profile: D1=8 / D2=9 / D3=7 / D4=9 / D5=10 / D6=9 / D7=8 (confidence: HIGH).
> Cycle position: D6 Phase 3 (Reality Testing) on [trigger event].
> CPCA: Composite AMBER (CPCA-1 AMBER, CPCA-2 AMBER, CPCA-3 GREEN, CPCA-4 GREEN).
> Forecast: Phase 4b retreat with Phase 5 victory declaration within 21–28 days, probability 65–75% (HIGH reduced to MODERATE-HIGH per CPCA).
> Falsifier: No D3 response within 10 days of [threshold] → degradation confirmed.

## Quick Reference Card

### Dimensions
D1 — Optionality (preserve choices, avoid commitment)
D2 — Coercion (prefer asymmetric pressure)
D3 — Popularity (the noncompensatory gate)
D4 — Constraint rejection (bypass institutions)
D5 — Zero-sum (every interaction has a winner)
D6 — Escalate-retreat (extreme open, reframed climbdown)
D7 — Loyalty (personal over institutional)

### Decision Cycle (7 steps)
Trigger → D5 frame → D2 option → D6 escalation → D3 screen → branch (advance via D4/D7, or retreat preserving D1) → narrative consolidation

### D6 Phases (6 sub-stages)
1. Extreme opening → 2. Spectacle amplification → 3. Reality testing → 4a. Advance / 4b. Retreat → 5. Victory declaration. Double Cycle: Phase 5 → Phase 1.

### CPCA Axes
Sleep · Language · Impulse-Strategic · Consequence Horizon. GREEN/AMBER/RED. Composite = worst axis, except two AMBER → RED.

### Degradation Hierarchy (most vulnerable first)
D3 → D6 retreat → D1 → D6 escalation → D5 → D7

### Predictability Paradox
The content of decisions varies; the structure is fixed. Forecast structure first, content second.

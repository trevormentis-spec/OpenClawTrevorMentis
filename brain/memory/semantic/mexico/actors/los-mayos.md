# Entity: Los Mayitos (La Mayiza / Mayiza Faction)

**Last updated:** 2026-05-17
**Last source date:** 2026-05-15 (Milenio, Infobae)
**Stale warning days:** 14
**Type:** actor (criminal faction)
**Primary themes:** cartel_security

## Description

Faction of the Sinaloa Cartel loyal to Ismael "El Mayo" Zambada. In violent internal
war with Los Chapitos since July 2024. Winning the territorial conflict as of May 2026.

### Ismael "El Mayo" Zambada García

El Mayo pleaded guilty in August 2025 in Brooklyn, admitting to co-founding CDS and
trafficking thousands of tons of drugs. He stated his business relied on "paying bribes
to police and military commanders and to politicians so they would allow us to operate
freely." [LA Times (A2), May 10]

His network of political, judicial, and military protections was uniquely personal — not
easily inherited by his son. The Mayiza faction has the tactical momentum but may lack
the godfather-level state protection that made the original CDS resilient.

## Recent developments

### Rocha Moya home attack (May 10, 2026)

Mayiza gunmen fired dozens of rounds into an abandoned Rocha Moya family home in Culiacán's
Las Quintas neighborhood. Breitbart Texas (C3) attributes the attack to Mayiza via Sinaloa
State authorities. The video shows four men exiting a sedan with rifles and spraying the wall.
The property had been uninhabited for over a decade — the attack is symbolic rather than
operational. [Breitbart Texas (C3), May 10]

### Territorial gains

Mayiza controls the majority of Culiacán metro area, Mazatlán, and Navolato as of May 2026.
Infobae (B2) describes the Mayiza as "rancheros curtidos" who infiltrated the city in
discrete vehicles, kidnapped low-ranking Chapitos operatives, extracted information under
torture, and worked up the chain of command. The more flamboyant Chapitos social-media
presence proved tactically disadvantageous. [Infobae (B2), May 14]

### Culiacán escalation — iconic incident

The Mayiza's highest-impact single operation was the killing of 20 Chapitos members, four
of whose decapitated bodies were hung from a bridge. A truck with the remaining 16 bodies
was abandoned nearby with a sign reading "Bienvenidos al nuevo Sinaloa" — insulting
Iván Archivaldo Guzmán by name. [Infobae (B2), May 14, citing FGR reports (A1)]

### Police targeting

The Mayiza has systematically targeted police officers aligned with or perceived as
aligned with the Chapitos-Rocha Moya security apparatus. Of the 80 police killed Sep
2024-Apr 2026, the majority in Culiacán (38 of 80) and Mazatlán (11 of 80), both Mayiza
strongholds. [Infobae/Revista Espejo (B2), May 7]

## Organizational assessment

**Tactical assessment:** Mayiza is winning the ground war through superior tactics —
disciplined, low-profile, methodical. They are not winning through superior firepower
or numbers. The social media assessment from WSJ was that analysts initially favored
Chapitos (more numerous, richer, 2,000 lookouts), but Mayiza's "ranchero" discipline
outlasted the "narco juniors" flamboyance. [Infobae (B2), May 14]

**Structural vulnerability:** El Mayo's personal network of state protection is not
inheritable. The Mayiza may win the street war but find themselves unable to operate
at the elite-political level that made the original CDS untouchable.

**Softener:** If Mayiza can co-opt or recruit from the surviving Chapitos political network
(former Rocha Moya officials), they inherit "state capture" capability. Some small signs
this is happening — defectors working for Mayiza per Ríodoce.
**Tightener:** If the Mayiza wins territorially but can't replace the lost political
protection, they become a conventional cartel (vulnerable to law enforcement) rather
than a state-captured one (invulnerable).

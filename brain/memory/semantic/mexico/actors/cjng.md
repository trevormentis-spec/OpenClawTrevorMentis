# Entity: Jalisco New Generation Cartel (CJNG)

**Last updated:** 2026-05-17
**Last source date:** 2026-05-16 (Proceso/ACLED)
**Stale warning days:** 14
**Type:** actor (criminal organization)
**Primary themes:** cartel_security, energy_infra

## Current Status

Active. Military power and criminal revenue streams intact despite leadership
decapitation. El Mencho killed February 22, 2026. Succession underway without
organizational fracture.

## Leadership Succession

### Predecessor: Nemesio Oseguera Cervantes "El Mencho"

Killed February 22, 2026, during a Mexican Army operation in Tapalpa, Jalisco.
[Proceso/ACLED (A2/B2), May 12 — multiple independent confirmations]

El Mencho's death was the single-point-of-failure vulnerability repeatedly identified
in analyst assessments. The CJNG's survival without fracture in the three months since
changes the framework assumption from "vulnerable to decapitation" to "institutionalized
enough to survive decapitation."

### Claimed successor: Juan Carlos Valencia González "El Pelón" / "El 03"

The Wall Street Journal reported on March 18, 2026, that Valencia González — a US citizen,
also known as "El Pelón," "El Tricky Tres," "03" — had assumed leadership. El País confirmed
on April 6. [Wikipedia (C3, citing WSJ A1 and El País A2)]

Valencia González is El Mencho's stepson. His US citizenship is a significant vulnerability
for the organization (exposes him to US prosecution).

### Captured contender: Audías Flores Silva "El Jardinero"

Captured April 27, 2026, by the Mexican Navy in Nayarit. Was a potential successor.
ACLED analyst Sandra Pellegrini describes his capture as a "tactical blow" with localized
effects, not organizational. [Proceso/ACLED (A2/B2), May 12; Washington Times (B2), May 14]

### Remaining contender: Gonzalo Mendoza Gaytán "El Sapo"

Still at large. No evidence of hostile competition with Valencia González for leadership.
[Proceso/ACLED (A2/B2), May 12]

## Organizational assessment (post-Mencho)

**ACLED (A2), May 12:** CJNG maintains intact military power and criminal markets.
"No evidence of fracture." Characterizes the current period as "tactical containment"
and organizational adaptation rather than decline.

**Chris Dalby/Dyami Security Intelligence (B2):** "Same thing that happens in any
multinational when the head is lost — the economic structure keeps operating."
Revenue from the Mencho-era portfolio (drugs, extortion, fuel contraband) continues unchanged.

**Softener:** If Valencia González is indicted in the US (his US citizenship makes
prosecution easier), CJNG could face a second leadership crisis within 12 months.
**Tightener:** If the current non-fracture pattern holds through Q3 2026, the framework
assumption must update: CJNG is institutionally resilient to decapitation.

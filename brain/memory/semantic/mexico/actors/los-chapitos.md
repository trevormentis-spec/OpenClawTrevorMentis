# Entity: Los Chapitos — Chapitos Faction

**Last updated:** 2026-05-17

**Stale warning days:** 14
**Type:** actor (criminal faction)
**Primary themes:** cartel_security, us_mexico

## Description

Faction of the Sinaloa Cartel led by sons of Joaquín "El Chapo" Guzmán. In violent
internal war with Los Mayitos since July 2024. In structural decline as of May 2026.

### Iván Archivaldo Guzmán Salazar (42)

The last Chapitos leader not in US custody. Has reportedly been in contact with US
authorities about a negotiated surrender for over a year. His lawyer, Jeffrey Lichtman,
has not commented. [LA Times (A2), May 10]

If Iván Archivaldo surrenders, Los Chapitos as an independent faction effectively
ceases to exist — the organization becomes a US-government-managed intelligence asset.

### Ovidio Guzmán López

Pleaded guilty July 2025. Cooperation agreement includes $80M forfeiture. His information
is believed to be the basis for the April 2026 Rocha Moya indictment. [Política Expansión
(B2), May 13; LA Times (A2), May 10]

### Joaquín Guzmán López (39)

Kidnapped El Mayo Zambada and delivered him to US authorities in July 2024. Pleaded guilty
December 2025, minimum 10 years. Told the judge he studied finance in college and entered
the drug trade because "his brothers are bullies." [LA Times (A2), May 10]

## Recent developments

### Structural collapse (May 2026)

**Ismael Bojórquez, director of Ríodoce (B3):** Chapitos commanders are "detenidos, están
muertos, y otros se voltearon contra él y ahora trabajan para los Mayitos." The Chapitos'
2,000 lookouts on motorcycles, 2,600 illegal cameras in Culiacán, and social media
influencer network have not prevented territorial losses. [Infobae/WSJ (B2/A1), May 14]

### Rocha Moya indictment (April-May 2026)

The US indictment of Governor Rocha Moya and nine officials, with specific allegations
that Iván Archivaldo commanded sicarios to terrorize voters in the 2021 election, exposed
the depth of Chapitos state-government penetration. [LA Times (A2), May 10]

### Police assassination wave

Milenio's Azul Cobalto (A2) data shows police killings in Sinaloa rose 1,900% (2 in 2023
to 40 in 2025). The increase correlates with the Chapitos-Mayiza rupture — police are
targeted as Chapitos assets by Mayiza hit squads. [Milenio (B3), May 15]

## Downside assessment

**Highly likely (80%):** Los Chapitos as an independent faction will not survive 2026.
Either Iván Archivaldo surrenders (ending the faction) or Mayiza territorial gains
reduce them to a non-competitive rump. The only open question is whether the faction's
US intelligence value (from cooperating brothers) is high enough to earn significant
sentencing reductions.

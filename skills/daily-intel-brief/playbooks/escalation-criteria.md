# Playbook: Escalation Criteria

When the daily flips into a special. The daily is a cadenced product;
specials are demand-driven. Knowing where the line sits prevents both
under-escalation (a Hormuz incident gets one paragraph in the daily and
the principal is blindsided 6 hours later) and over-escalation (every
minor wire becomes a special and the daily product loses its meaning).

---

## Escalate to a same-day special if any of:

1. **Kinetic threshold:** A state-on-state kinetic exchange with
   confirmed casualties (A1 or B1 rated) and at least one of the
   participants is a major power (US, UK, FR, DE, RU, CN) or a treaty
   ally of one.

2. **Strategic surprise:** A development the daily would not have
   predicted at >35% probability — examples: a coup, a no-notice
   mobilisation, a treaty withdrawal, a sovereign default.

3. **Principal's equity:** A development that materially affects a
   named principal interest (maintained in `~/.openclaw/principals.json`).
   Default examples (override per principal): Strait of Hormuz closure,
   SWIFT-level financial sanctions, NATO Article 5 invocation, Israeli
   ground operation outside Gaza/Lebanon.

4. **Cross-region cascade:** A development whose second-order effects
   span ≥3 of the six regions within the next 7 days. Example: a major
   Saudi oil facility strike (Middle East kinetic, North America gas
   prices, Asia LNG flows, Europe inflation, Global Finance vol spike).

5. **Falsification of yesterday's BLUF:** Yesterday's headline judgment
   has been overtaken by events. The principal needs to know
   immediately, not at 06:30 tomorrow.

---

## Same-day special structure (4 pages, 60 minutes)

Same skeleton as the daily's regional section, scoped to the triggering
issue:

1. Cover with new BLUF.
2. What changed (new evidence in last N hours).
3. What the daily had said (and where the daily was right / wrong /
   silent).
4. Updated key judgments with bands and 7-day predictions.
5. Updated I&W board for the issue.
6. Recommended principal actions (only if asked; otherwise omit).

Generated by re-running this skill in `--mode special --topic <slug>`,
which:

- Skips the per-region pass.
- Sends the analyst a focused incident set on the topic.
- Produces a single-section product, not a six-section one.
- Preserves the daily's scheduled run for the same morning.

---

## Do NOT escalate for:

- Wire-only chatter without primary corroboration. The daily's narrative
  is the right home for "we are watching this".
- Forecasts of tomorrow's daily. (Let it land in tomorrow's daily.)
- Events that move markets without affecting principal equities. (The
  Global Finance section handles those.)
- Events the principal has explicitly de-prioritised in their standing
  guidance.

---

## On principal restraint

It is tempting to escalate often because escalating feels like
helpfulness. It is not. A principal who receives a special every other
day stops opening them — and then misses the one that mattered. The
daily's calibrated bands are the protocol; the special is reserved for
the cases the daily's structure would have under-served.

When in doubt, run the special anyway, but send it as an annex to the
next daily rather than as a stand-alone push. That way the principal
sees it within ~24h and the daily's signal-to-noise stays clean.

---

## Stop conditions during a special

- The triggering event is no longer the headline within 6 hours →
  fold the work into tomorrow's daily as an extended regional section.
- A second special-grade event arrives → write the second one separately;
  do not bundle. Bundles obscure both.
- Three consecutive specials in 48 hours on the same topic → stand up
  a standing watch product on that topic; stop running the special as a
  one-off.

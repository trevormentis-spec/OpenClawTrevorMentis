The June 2026 contract (expiring 1 June 2026) trades at $0.09-$0.10 bid-ask, implying approximately 9-10% probability. The July 2026 contract shows 19-20% probability. The August 2026 contract reaches 29-30%. The January 2027 contract commands 54-56% probability. This monotonic increase across time horizons is consistent with a diffusion process—market participants expect incremental progress rather than a sudden breakthrough.

The volume distribution is notable: the January 2027 contract has $733,335 in volume, while the June 2026 contract has $1,331,675. The higher volume on the near-term contract despite lower probability suggests hedging activity. Sophisticated traders may be buying the June contract as a tail-risk hedge against an unexpected rapid agreement, while concentrating directional bets on the January 2027 expiry.

The KXTRUMPIRAN contract (trading at $0.01-$0.01, implying 1% probability) is particularly informative. This contract likely references a specific Trump-Iran event—possibly a meeting or direct negotiation. The near-zero probability indicates the market assigns essentially no chance to a Trump-led breakthrough in the current political environment. This is consistent with the broader narrative that any agreement will be negotiated through official diplomatic channels, not personal diplomacy.

## Alternative Analysis

**Hypothesis A: The market is wrong—agreement will come sooner than priced.** The 9-10% probability on June 2026 may underestimate the impact of oil market pressure. If the "point of no return" analysis is correct, diplomatic urgency could accelerate dramatically. The market may be extrapolating from recent slow progress without accounting for potential inflection points. This hypothesis would be confirmed by a rapid increase in the June contract price above $0.20.

**Hypothesis B: The market is wrong—no agreement will be reached by January 2027.** The 54-56% probability may overestimate the likelihood of resolution. Structural obstacles—Supreme Leader Khamenei's opposition, Israeli pressure on the US administration, Gulf state concerns—could derail negotiations. The market may be pricing in a "peace premium" that reflects wishful thinking rather than realistic assessment. This hypothesis would be confirmed by the January 2027 contract falling below $0.40.

**Hypothesis C: The market is correctly pricing a framework agreement but not final implementation.** The probability surface may reflect expectations of an interim or framework agreement by January 2027, with final implementation extending into 2028. The KXUSAIRANAGREEMENT contract language may be ambiguous enough to encompass partial agreements. This hypothesis would be confirmed by clarification of contract terms or by a framework announcement that does not resolve all outstanding issues.

**What would change TREVOR's mind:** A sudden spike in the June 2026 contract above $0.25 would indicate new information entering the market and would require reassessment of the timeline. Conversely, a sustained drop in the January 2027 contract below $0.40 would suggest the market is losing confidence in any agreement.

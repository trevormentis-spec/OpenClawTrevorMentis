## Alternative Analysis

**Hypothesis A (Null Hypothesis):** The absence of intelligence reports is due to collection failure rather than deliberate information denial. Under this hypothesis, the AES states have not implemented a coordinated information denial campaign; rather, intelligence collection capabilities have degraded due to resource constraints or technical issues.

**Evidence For:** No new intelligence reports have been received from any source in the Sahel theatre for five consecutive cycles. This could indicate a systemic collection failure rather than deliberate suppression.

**Evidence Against:** The previous assessment identified specific indicators of information denial, including the suspension of commercial flights at Bamako-Sénou International Airport and the absence of Goïta from public appearances. These indicators are consistent with deliberate information denial rather than collection failure. Additionally, the simultaneous suppression of reporting across three countries suggests coordination rather than independent failures.

**Assessment:** TREVOR assesses Hypothesis A as **unlikely [15-25%]**. The specific indicators identified in the previous assessment are more consistent with deliberate information denial than collection failure.

**Hypothesis B (Internal Instability):** The absence of intelligence reports is due to internal instability within AES states, particularly Mali, rather than deliberate information denial. Under this hypothesis, the Goïta regime is facing a coup attempt or severe internal crisis that has disrupted normal reporting channels.

**Evidence For:** Goïta's absence from public appearances since April 27 (now 14 days) and the continued suspension of commercial flights at Bamako-Sénou International Airport are consistent with internal instability.

**Evidence Against:** The information denial campaign appears coordinated across three countries, not limited to Mali. If the cause were internal instability in Mali, one would expect reporting from Niger and Burkina Faso to continue. The simultaneous suppression of reporting across all three countries suggests coordination rather than independent crises.

**Assessment:** TREVOR assesses Hypothesis B as **unlikely [20-30%]**. While internal instability in Mali is a contributing factor, it does not explain the simultaneous suppression of reporting across Niger and Burkina Faso.

**Hypothesis C (Strategic Realignment):** The absence of intelligence reports is due to deliberate information denial by AES states to provide cover for Niger's strategic realignment toward JNIM. Under this hypothesis, the AES leadership is aware of Niger's engagement with JNIM and is suppressing reporting to prevent external intervention or internal dissent.

**Evidence For:** The coordinated nature of the information denial campaign, the absence of any denial from Niamey, and the absence of any AES joint statement reaffirming solidarity are all consistent with this hypothesis.

**Evidence Against:** None identified in the current cycle.

**Assessment:** TREVOR assesses Hypothesis C as **highly likely [70-85%]**. This hypothesis best explains the available evidence and is consistent with the trajectory established in the previous assessment.

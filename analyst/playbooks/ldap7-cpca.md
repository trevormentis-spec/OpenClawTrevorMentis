# LDAP-7 CPCA Overlay — Scoring Playbook

**Purpose:** Assess whether a leader retains the cognitive capacity to execute predicted behavioural patterns.
**Rule:** CPCA modifies confidence, not direction. It is a confidence-level adjustment, not a replacement for dimension scores.

---

## CPCA-1: Sleep & Circadian Patterns

**Target:** Detect executive-function degradation from sleep deficit.

### Scoring Protocol
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| **GREEN** | Communications during normal waking hours; no visible fatigue markers; no staff accounts of sleep disruption |
| **AMBER** | Multiple communications between 0200–0600 local; visible fatigue in appearances; staff reports of reduced sleep |
| **RED** | Sustained pattern of late-night activity (>5 days); objective markers of sleep deprivation; hospitalisation or medical incidents |

### Data Sources
- Public communication time-stamps (Truth Social, X/Twitter, Weibo, press conferences)
- Photograph/video analysis for fatigue markers (periorbital darkening, speech slurring)
- Pool reports, staff leaks, WHCA / Kremlin pool notes
- Medical incident reporting

---

## CPCA-2: Linguistic & Cognitive Markers

**Target:** Detect processing-capacity changes via longitudinal content analysis.

### Scoring Protocol
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| **GREEN** | Scripted-spontaneous divergence within normal baseline; no increase in lexical errors; coherence metrics stable |
| **AMBER** | Notable increase in non-sequiturs or word-finding difficulty; scripted material significantly outperforms spontaneous speech; topic persistence degradation |
| **RED** | Marked lexical simplification; repetition loops; incoherent sentence structure; loss of name/date recall in spontaneous speech; verbal perseveration |

### Data Sources
- Long-form interviews (full transcripts, not clips)
- Press conference Q&A vs. prepared remarks
- Teleprompter vs. off-teleprompter segments
- Lexical diversity metrics (type-token ratio, hapax ratio)

---

## CPCA-3: Impulse-Strategic Ratio

**Target:** Detect shift from strategic coercion to reactive self-undermining.

### Scoring Protocol
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| **GREEN** | Coercive actions serve identifiable strategic objectives; no attacks on allies whose cooperation is currently needed |
| **AMBER** | Occasional self-undermining actions: attacking a key ally, foreclosing a required option without strategic gain |
| **RED** | Sustained pattern of self-undermining coercion; ally attacks during active negotiations; statements that foreclose multiple necessary options simultaneously |

### Key Indicator
**Strongest diagnostic cue:** Attacking an ally whose cooperation is needed for an active negotiation. This is almost never strategic.

---

## CPCA-4: Consequence Horizon

**Target:** Detect degradation in capacity to model 2nd- and 3rd-order effects.

### Scoring Protocol
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| **GREEN** | Decisions show evidence of downstream consequences being considered; no 48h contradictory directives |
| **AMBER** | Occasional contradictory directives within 48h; decisions that produce predictable negative outcomes the subject did not anticipate |
| **RED** | Sustained pattern of self-undermining decisions; last-person-in-the-room reversals; decisions that visibly harm the subject's own stated objectives |

### Key Indicator
**Strongest diagnostic cue:** Last-person-in-the-room reversal — the leader agrees to one position, then reverses after meeting with a different advisor, with no new information.

## Composite Scoring

| Axis Configuration | Composite | Effect |
|-------------------|-----------|--------|
| All axes GREEN | **GREEN** | Predictions unmodified |
| One axis AMBER, rest GREEN | **AMBER** | −1 confidence level; timing windows +30% |
| Two+ axes AMBER, OR any RED | **RED** | −2 confidence levels; timing unreliable; escalation-without-retreat flagged |

### Degradation-Aware Forecasting

When CPCA composite is RED, apply this override to the LDAP Decision Cycle:

1. **D3 (Popularity Screen)** is likely non-functional → branch-point monitoring is unreliable
2. **D6 Retreat Phase** may be unreachable → the leader escalates but cannot execute Phase 4b
3. **D6 Escalation Phase** remains active → semi-automatic, overlearned
4. **Double Cycle probability doubles** without the D3 brake to terminate Cycle A

**Predicted outcome under RED CPCA:** Escalation without off-ramp. Not paralysis — aggression that cannot self-terminate.

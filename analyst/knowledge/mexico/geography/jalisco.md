# Geography: Jalisco

**Type:** State (West-Central, Pacific)
**Themes:** cartel_security, economy_markets, worldcup_travel
**Capital:** Guadalajara
**Population:** ~8.3 million (3rd largest state)

## Economic Profile
- Guadalajara metro: Mexico's 2nd largest economy, tech hub (Mexico's Silicon Valley)
- Tequila: $4B+ annual industry — DO protected, major export
- Agriculture: agave, berries, corn
- Manufacturing: electronics, automotive components, aerospace (Bombardier facility)
- Logistics: Pacific port of Puerto Vallarta, Guadalajara international airport
- Tech: Oracle, HP, IBM, Amazon have significant operations in Guadalajara
- World Cup 2026: Guadalajara is a host venue (Estadio Akron, 4 matches)

## Political Control
- Governor: Pablo Lemus Navarro (Movimiento Ciudadano, since Dec 2024)
- Previous governor: Enrique Alfaro (MC, 2018-2024)
- Strong MC presence — Jalisco is the party's base
- Tension with federal Morena government on security coordination

## Criminal Control
- **CJNG HQ:** Jalisco is CJNG's home state. Guadalajara metro is operational center
- CJNG penetration: Jalisco state security apparatus is the most compromised in Mexico
- Extortion: Guadalajara metro businesses regularly pay piso tax
- CJNG presence everywhere but concentrated in Guadalajara, Puerto Vallarta, and the Tequila/Jalisco highlands corridor
- CJNG controls Manzanillo port adjacency (Colima-Jalisco border, Manzanillo is Mexico's busiest Pacific port)

## Key Locations
- **Guadalajara metro:** CJNG operational HQ. Zapopan (Lemus was mayor), Tlaquepaque, Tonalá. CJNG operates openly but with lower violence than Culiacán
- **Puerto Vallarta:** Major tourist destination. CJNG presence for drug trafficking and money laundering. Tourist extortion moderate risk
- **Tepalcatepec corridor:** Jalisco-Michoacán border. Active battlefront with Cárteles Unidos
- **Tapalpa:** Site of El Mencho's death (Feb 22, 2026). Pueblo Mágico
- **Lagos de Moreno:** Jalisco highlands. Highway nexus for trafficking routes north
- **Manzanillo (Colima border):** Mexico's busiest Pacific port. CJNG/at least some Sinaloa contestation

## World Cup 2026 Considerations
- Estadio Akron (Guadalajara): 4 group-stage matches scheduled
- Venue security: CJNG rarely targets tourists (bad for business), but extortion of hotels/restaurants is sustained
- Transit from airport to venue — narco-blockades on peripheral roads during operations
- Puerto Vallarta: inbound US tourist flows, potential soft target
- State-level security coordination with federal government is the key variable

## Infrastructure
- CFE western grid — significant demand from Guadalajara industrial corridor
- Guadalajara-Aguascalientes highway — commercial corridor, occasional cartel interdiction
- Puerto Vallarta airport — international tourism gateway
- Manzanillo port complex (in adjacent Colima) — critical for Pacific trade
- Pipeline infrastructure: Salamanca-León pipeline (huachicoleo target in northern Jalisco-Guanajuato border)

# Geography: Michoacán

**Type:** State (Southwest, Pacific)
**Themes:** cartel_security, energy_infra
**Capital:** Morelia
**Population:** ~4.7 million

## Economic Profile
- Avocado: $2B+ annual export industry — cartel extortion target (piso tax on every hectare)
- Mining: iron ore, copper — illegal mining and extortion by CU and CJNG
- Agriculture: lemons, limes, berries, mangoes — high-value extortion targets
- Port of Lázaro Cárdenas: major Pacific container port, fentanyl precursor entry point, iron ore export
- Remittances: ~$1.5B/year — significant economic flow
- Forest products: illegal logging by organized crime

## Political Control
- Morena governor: Alfredo Ramírez Bedolla (since 2021)
- State government historically more independent from federal control than Sinaloa
- Local power structures highly fragmented — municipal autonomy is the reality

## Criminal Control
- **CJNG:** Dominant in the north and coastal corridor. Controls Lázaro Cárdenas port access. Expanding into Tierra Caliente (southern Michoacán)
- **Cárteles Unidos:** Defensive control of Tepalcatepec / Tierra Caliente. Coalition of La Familia Michoacana remnants, Viagras, Caballeros Templarios remnants
- **La Nueva Familia Michoacana:** Distinct from CU but overlaps in some areas
- **Territorial battle zone:** Tierra Caliente (Aguililla, Apatzingán, Coalcomán, Tepalcatepec) — most active front in Mexico's cartel wars

## Key Locations
- **Apatzingán:** Tierra Caliente hub. Hot climate, drug production, strategic to both CJNG and CU
- **Aguililla:** Birthplace of El Mencho. CJNG stronghold. Site of repeated military operations
- **Uruapan:** Second-largest city. Avocado center. CJNG/CU contestation zone
- **Tepalcatepec:** Cárteles Unidos HQ and defensive anchor
- **Morelia:** State capital. Relatively lower violence but CJNG influence present
- **Lázaro Cárdenas:** Major Pacific port. Fentanyl precursor entry, iron ore export, illegal logging hub
- **Zitácuaro:** Eastern Michoacán, gateway to Estado de México — CJNG corridor

## Recent Incident Pattern
- Tierra Caliente: daily violence, police assassinations, narco-blockades
- Avocado-producing municipalities: extortion-related violence, land-grabbing
- IED use against military patrols in CU areas (notable innovation)
- Lázaro Cárdenas port: container theft, customs corruption

## Infrastructure
- Lázaro Cárdenas port — strategic national infrastructure
- CFE hydroelectric plants (Infiernillo dam)
- Agua Fría-Guadalajara pipeline (huachicoleo target)
- Avocado export cold chain infrastructure

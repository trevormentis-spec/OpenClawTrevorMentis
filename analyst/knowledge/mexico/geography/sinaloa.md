# Geography: Sinaloa

**Type:** State (Northwest Pacific)
**Themes:** cartel_security, political_risk
**Population:** ~3 million
**Capital:** Culiacán

## What Sits There
- **Culiacán:** Sinaloa cartel operational center. Site of multiple combat episodes (2019, 2023, 2025)
- **Mazatlán:** Major Pacific port, tourist destination, cartel money laundering hub
- **Los Mochis:** Agricultural hub, Chapitos territorial base
- **Sierra Madre Occidental:** Opium poppy / synthetic drug lab country. Fentanyl precursor routes from Pacific coast

## Economically
- Agriculture (tomatoes, mangoes, chili peppers) — $3B+ annual exports
- Aquaculture (shrimp) — major industry in Mazatlán
- Drug trafficking — estimated $3-5B annually (fentanyl, meth, heroin, cocaine transit)
- Tourism (Mazatlán) — limited, cartel violence supresses sector
- Port of Mazatlán — containerized cargo, cruise ships, fishing fleet

## Politically
- Morena governor: Rubén Rocha Moya (since 2021), allied with AMLO/Sheinbaum
- Chapitos have significant influence over local officials
- History of narco-politics: from the Arellano Félix/Beltran Leyva era through current Chapitos-Mayos factional war

## Criminally
- **Sinaloa Cartel HQ** — origin of all major Sinaloa-aligned factions
- Internal civil war: Los Chapitos vs Los Mayos (ongoing)
- Key corridor for fentanyl precursors entering from Pacific
- Opium poppy production in Sierra Madre — traditional base
- Culiacán: site of repeated military-cartel confrontations (city lockdown episodes)

## Watch Items
- Mayor-level official assassinations — signal of Chapitos-Mayos factional escalation
- Culiacán/Sinaloa highway narco-blockades (narcobloqueos) — coordination test
- Mazatlán cruise traffic cancellations — cartel violence spillover into tourism

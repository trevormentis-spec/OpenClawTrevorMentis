# Geography: Tamaulipas

**Type:** State (Northeast, US border)
**Themes:** cartel_security, us_mexico, worldcup_travel
**Capital:** Ciudad Victoria
**Population:** ~3.5 million

## Economic Profile
- Border manufacturing (maquiladoras): Reynosa and Matamoros are major manufacturing hubs
- Energy: Burgos Basin natural gas fields, Pemex pipelines, Eagle Ford Shale adjacency
- Agriculture: sorghum, cotton, cattle — northern Tamaulipas
- Ports: Tampico (Gulf), Altamira (major container/chemical port)
- Trade: Land ports of entry at Nuevo Laredo (#1 US-Mexico commercial crossing), Reynosa, Matamoros
- Migration: Primary corridor for migrant flows toward Texas
- Remittances: ~$800M/year — family support from US-based Tamaulipas diaspora

## Political Control
- Morena governor: Américo Villarreal Anaya (since 2022)
- Tamaulipas has a history of narco-politics: PRI dominance collapsed as CDN/Zetas gained influence
- Local government administration heavily penetrated at municipal level

## Criminal Control
- **CDN (Cártel del Noreste):** Dominant in Nuevo Laredo, Reynosa, Matamoros. Controls border crossing logistics
- **Zetas Vieja Escuela:** Aligned with Gulf Cartel, contesting some CDN positions
- **Gulf Cartel (CDG):** Historically dominant, now reduced to pockets around Tampico and some border areas
- Border control: CDN effectively taxes all cross-border commerce at Nuevo Laredo
- Migrant smuggling primary revenue stream under Trump migration enforcement surge
- CDN operates more like a border franchise than a territorial expansionist cartel

## Key Locations
- **Nuevo Laredo:** #1 US-Mexico commercial crossing (~40% of overland trade). CDN operational HQ. Most strategically important border city
- **Reynosa:** Major maquiladora center. CDN-controlled. Occasionally contested by Gulf/Zetas Vieja Escuela
- **Matamoros:** Third border crossing. Site of historic Gulf Cartel power. Now CDN-dominant
- **Tampico/Altamira:** Gulf coast oil and port zone. Gulf Cartel presence. Lower violence than border cities
- **Ciudad Victoria:** State capital. More government presence, lower criminal visibility

## Incident Pattern
- Border city violence is localized rather than state-wide — CDN manages violence to maintain operational stability
- Nuevo Laredo: occasional SEDENA-CDN confrontations, armed convoy movements
- Reynosa/Matamoros: lower violence than historic peaks
- Extra-judicial: Tamaulipas has one of Mexico's highest forced disappearance rates

## World Cup 2026 Considerations
- No direct venues (Monterrey is nearest host)
- Transit corridor: World Cup tourists to Monterrey would pass through or near Tamaulipas if driving
- Air travel via Monterrey avoids ground transit risk
- Direct impact: reputation risk — "Tamaulipas is dangerous" narrative affects overall Mexico tourism perception

## Infrastructure
- Nuevo Laredo World Trade Bridge — primary commercial port of entry
- Reynosa-Monterrey highway — key commercial corridor
- Altamira port — container/chemical, some energy exports
- Pemex Burgos Basin gas fields and pipelines
- Ferromex rail line — intermodal freight through Nuevo Laredo

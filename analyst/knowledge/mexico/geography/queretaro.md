# Geography: Querétaro

**Type:** State (Bajío region, central Mexico)
**Themes:** energy_infra, economy_markets, cartel_security
**Capital:** Santiago de Querétaro
**Population:** ~2.4 million

## Economic Profile
- Mexico's fastest-growing state economy (averaging 4-5% growth)
- Aerospace hub: Bombardier (major facility), Safran, Airbus suppliers
- Automotive: transmission and engine plants, assembly
- **Data center corridor:** KIO Networks, Equinix, AWS, Microsoft — Querétaro is Mexico's #1 data center hub
- Manufacturing: white goods, electronics, food processing
- Querétaro industrial belt: one of Mexico's most dynamic manufacturing zones
- Education: Strong universities (UNAM Juriquilla campus, ITESM, UAQ)

## Political Control
- PAN governors historically — Querétaro was a PAN stronghold
- Current governor: Mauricio Kuri González (PAN, since 2021)
- Political counterweight to Morena's national dominance
- Opposition to federal energy policy (prefers open energy market, not CFE/Pemex primacy)

## Criminal Control
- **Historically low violence for Mexico** — Querétaro was one of the safest states
- **Under pressure:** CJNG and Santa Rosa de Lima Cartel (Guanajuato) encroachment on Querétaro's periphery
- **Extortion:** Growing risk for data center operators and industrial park tenants
- KFR: Kidnappings for ransom targeting wealthy Querétaro families
- Data center security: physical perimeter security is high but employee transit routes and extortion risk sustained
- Cartel presence is NOT territorial control — it's opportunistic exploitation

## Key Locations
- **Querétaro Industrial Belt:** KIO Networks data center cluster, aerospace park, automotive suppliers
- **Querétaro City Centro:** Colonial downtown, tourist destination, business center
- **Juriquilla:** UNAM campus, tech zone, KIO secondary presence
- **El Marqués:** Industrial corridor, growing manufacturing zone
- **San Juan del Río:** Secondary industrial city, highway nexus
- **Highway 57 (Mexico City-Querétaro):** Major commercial corridor, occasional cargo theft

## Data Center Infrastructure (Critical)
- Querétaro holds ~40% of Mexico's data center capacity (est.)
- Attracted by: stable grid (vs. CDMX blackout risk), fiber connectivity, physical security reputation, tax incentives
- KIO Networks operates largest cluster in the Querétaro industrial corridor
- AWS and Microsoft have significant cloud node presence
- Energy dependency: data center power demand strains CFE regional grid
- Water dependency: cooling requires significant water — growing concern given Bajío drought conditions
- **Primary risk factors:** Cartel extortion, CFE grid reliability, water scarcity, highway cargo theft for equipment deliveries

## Infrastructure
- Querétaro-CDMX highway (57D) — busiest commercial corridor in central Mexico
- Querétaro International Airport — growing cargo and passenger
- CFE Bajío transmission corridor — data center demand strain
- KIO data center complex — dedicated substations, backup generators
- Water infrastructure: reliance on groundwater aquifers, decreasing water table

## Watch Items
- Data center security incident (extortion, theft, employee targeting) — would be high-profile
- Querétaro homicide/SESNSP trend increase — would signal cartel encroachment acceleration
- CFE power outage affecting data center uptime — investment climate signal
- KIO/equinix expansion announcements — confidence signal
- Water scarcity measures — could constrain data center expansion

## Background
- USMCA replaced NAFTA effective July 1, 2020
- Mandatory joint review every 6 years (Art. 34.7)
- Review determines whether agreement continues, is amended, or begins sunset process
- Trump administration (2025-2029) has signaled aggressive posture on:
  - Chinese transshipment via Mexico
  - Unilateral US FTO designations and their impact on trade
  - Border security conditionality in trade terms

## What to Watch
1. US tariff actions against Mexico before/during review — Trump using leverage
2. Mexican response posture — sovereignty defense vs pragmatic negotiation
3. Automotive rules of origin changes — would impact ~$60B in annual US-Mexico auto trade
4. Digital trade provisions — data localization requirements
5. Fentanyl enforcement built into trade terms — new conditionality

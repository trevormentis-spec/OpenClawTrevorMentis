# Actor: KIO Networks / Octavio Camarena

**Role:** Mexico's largest data center operator / CEO
**HQ:** Monterrey, Mexico
**Theme:** energy_infra, economy_markets

## Who
KIO Networks is Mexico's leading data center and digital infrastructure company. Founded by Octavio Camarena (CEO), KIO operates data centers across Mexico and Latin America. The Querétaro data center cluster (KIO's largest) is a critical piece of Mexico's digital infrastructure strategy.

## Why They Matter
- Mexico's digital backbone — hosts cloud infrastructure for government, banks, and US tech companies
- Querétaro data center corridor is a major energy consumer — strains CFE grid capacity
- Security risk: cartel extortion of data center operators, energy theft affecting uptime
- Data sovereignty implications: Mexican data staying in Mexico vs cloud exits to US
- KIO MEX8 (CDMX) announced March 2026 — expansion signal

## What They Control
- ~40% of Mexico's data center capacity (estimated)
- Querétaro's Querétaro Industrial Belt data center cluster
- CDMX metro data centers
- Latin American expansion (Colombia, Chile, Panama)

## What They Want
- Reliable power supply for data center operations (CFE grid improvements)
- Protection from cartel extortion/KFR in Querétaro and other locations
- Regulatory clarity on data sovereignty
- Continued nearshoring-driven demand for Mexican data infrastructure

## Risk Factors
- Huachicoleo/fuel theft affecting CFE generation capacity
- Cartel extortion targeting data center operators and employees
- Water-intensive cooling requirements vs water scarcity in Querétaro
- Physical security of facilities near cartel-controlled areas

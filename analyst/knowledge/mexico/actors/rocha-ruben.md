# Actor: Rubén Rocha Moya

**Role:** Governor of Sinaloa (Morena)
**Status:** On leave — US indictment April 29, 2026. Stepped down May 2, 2026 pending investigation.
**Age:** ~67
**Theme:** cartel_security, political_risk

## What Happened
On April 29, 2026, the US Department of Justice indicted Rocha Moya along with 9 other current and former Mexican officials for conspiracy with the Sinaloa Cartel. Accused of accepting bribes and electoral support from the Chapitos faction in exchange for protection and impunity. The indictment names Iván Archivaldo Guzmán as directing violence to influence the 2021 Sinaloa election on Rocha's behalf.

Rocha stepped down on May 2, 2026 (temporary leave) as a local investigation proceeds. He denies wrongdoing. His case has triggered a Morena internal crisis: the AMLO faction vehemently opposes any cooperation with US extradition; younger reformers see this as an opportunity to combat corruption within the party.

## Why He Matters
- The first sitting Mexican governor indicted by the US for drug trafficking
- The political test case for Sheinbaum's independence from AMLO
- If the US provides evidence, Rocha would be the highest-profile Mexican official prosecuted for cartel collusion
- If the case stalls, it signals that state penetration of Morena at the gubernatorial level has no consequences

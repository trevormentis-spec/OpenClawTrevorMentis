# Actor: Los Chapitos (Sinaloa Cartel Faction)

**Aliases:** Los Chapitos, Chapitos faction, Sinaloa Cartel — Guzmán faction
**Leader:** Iván Archivaldo Guzmán Salazar ("El Chapito")
**Status:** Active — under maximum pressure from US and Mexican authorities
**Themes:** cartel_security, us_mexico

## Who
The sons of Joaquín "El Chapo" Guzmán Loera who inherited his faction of the Sinaloa Cartel. Originally four brothers, now reduced:
- **Iván Archivaldo Guzmán Salazar** — the current operational leader, still at large. Allegedly ordered violent actions to influence 2021 Sinaloa election. Reports (May 12, 2026 LA Times) suggest he is seeking a deal with US authorities.
- **Jesús Alfredo Guzmán Salazar** — status uncertain, less public profile than Iván
- **Ovidio Guzmán López** — captured in Culiacán (January 5, 2023, second attempt after the "Culiacanazo" 2019), now in US custody
- **Joaquín Guzmán López** — captured July 2024 alongside El Mayo Zambada in El Paso, now in US custody

## Key Events
- **2019:** Ovidio captured briefly, released under cartel pressure ("Culiacanazo") — humiliation for AMLO government
- **2023:** Ovidio recaptured in second Culiacán operation, extradited to US
- **2023-2024:** Internal CDS war intensifies as Chapitos and Mayos compete for control
- **July 2024:** Joaquín Guzmán López allegedly lures El Mayo onto a plane to El Paso, both captured by US authorities. Massive controversy about whether the US deceived Mexico (the "kidnapping" narrative vs voluntary surrender)
- **June 2025:** OFAC sanctions Chapitos as a faction within the Sinaloa Cartel (designated as FTO/SDGT)
- **May 2026:** US indictment of Sinaloa Governor Rubén Rocha for collusion with Sinaloa Cartel — names Iván Archivaldo as key figure who ordered violence in 2021 election
- **May 12, 2026:** LA Times reports Iván Archivaldo may be seeking a cooperation deal with US

## What They Want
- Survival of the family-controlled drug empire
- Maintain fentanyl supply chain dominance (their primary revenue stream)
- Protect remaining leadership from US capture/kill operations
- Retain territorial control in Sinaloa (Culiacán, Los Mochis) against Mayos faction
- Possibly negotiate a plea deal — dramatically different from earlier posture

## What They Control
- Sinaloa Cartel's fentanyl trafficking infrastructure — precursors → production → US distribution
- Territory in Sinaloa (Culiacán metro area, Los Mochis) — contested with Mayos
- Corridor connections to US distribution networks (Los Angeles, Chicago, Atlanta, NYC, Philadelphia)
- Traditional opium poppy and heroin production routes in Sierra Madre
- Some local government relationships in Sinaloa (the Rocha indictment is the key evidence)

## What They Fear
- US capture or kill operations (CIA Ground Branch precedent, $10M reward on Iván)
- Mayos faction exploiting their brothers' incarceration to seize territory
- Mexican military operations (SEDENA) under Harfuch's direction
- Complete destruction of family control — Iván seeking a deal suggests this is their current assessment
- Asset forfeiture and financial network dismantlement

## Observable Indicators
- Iván Guzmán public appearances (or absence) — any sighting or confirmed location
- Rocha extradition/investigation progress — Chapitos state penetration exposed
- Culiacán narcobloqueo frequency — kinetic response to pressure indicator
- Fentanyl seizure volumes on Chapitos-controlled routes — operational capacity proxy
- Deal-seeking signals — lawyer visits to DOJ, surrender rumors
- Intra-Sinaloa violence between Chapitos and Mayos factions

## Key Distinction
The Chapitos are NOT the same as the Sinaloa Cartel. They are one faction. The Mayos are the other. The Sinaloa Cartel is a brand; the internal war is real and active.

# Actor: Los Mayos (Sinaloa Cartel Faction)

**Aliases:** Mayos faction, Zambada faction, Sinaloa Cartel — Zambada wing
**Founder/Historic Leader:** Ismael "El Mayo" Zambada García (captured July 2024, sentenced to life Jan 2026)
**Current Leadership:** Uncertain — likely Ismael Zambada Imperial ("El Mayito Gordo") or surviving Zambada family/associates
**Status:** Active — contesting Sinaloa control with Chapitos
**Themes:** cartel_security

## Who
The faction of the Sinaloa Cartel loyal to Ismael Zambada García, who co-founded the CDS with El Chapo in the 1980s. El Mayo was the more low-profile, business-focused half of the partnership — known for avoiding violence and focusing on logistics, corruption networks, and operational efficiency.

## Key Events
- **July 25, 2024:** El Mayo captured after being lured onto a private plane to El Paso by Joaquín Guzmán López (El Chapo's son). Massive controversy — Mexican government claims the US deceived Mexico. El Mayo's version: he was kidnapped.
- **August 2025:** El Mayo pleads guilty in Brooklyn federal court to continuing criminal enterprise and RICO charges
- **January 2026:** Sentenced to life in prison
- **2024-2026:** Post-capture, the Mayos faction enters leadership transition. Key Zambada family lieutenants had already been captured over the preceding decade:
  - Vicente Zambada Niebla ("El Vicentillo") — captured, cooperated with US, testified against El Chapo (2019)
  - Ismael Zambada Imperial ("El Mayito Gordo") — captured, served time, released — possible current leader
  - Serafín Zambada Ortíz — arrested in Arizona 2013, released 2018
  - Jesús "El Rey" Zambada — brother, captured, cooperated, key witness in El Chapo trial

## What They Want
- Maintain control of traditional Mayos revenue corridors (opium/heroin in Sierra Madre, cocaine transit via Pacific)
- Prevent Chapitos from taking over their territorial holdings
- Preserve the business-over-violence operational culture that distinguished Mayos from Chapitos
- Manage leadership succession without internal fracture

## What They Control
- Traditional opium/heroin production zones in Sinaloa's Sierra Madre
- Cocaine transit routes from South America via Pacific
- Some of El Mayo's extensive bribery/corruption networks (reportedly spent $1M/month on bribes)
- Territory in southern Sinaloa (Mazatlán area, Sierra foothills) and parts of Durango
- Financial networks built over four decades

## What They Fear
- Chapitos exploiting El Mayo's capture to seize Mayos territory through violence
- Succession vacuum causing internal fragmentation
- US FTO designation enabling direct kinetic action against remaining leadership
- Complete loss of the Sinaloa Cartel brand to Chapitos
- Asset forfeiture of their financial infrastructure

## Observable Indicators
- Intra-CDS violence location and frequency — which areas of Sinaloa are contested
- Mayos faction negotiations with CJNG for alliance against Chapitos
- Any public messaging (narcomantas) claiming factional responsibility
- DEA/OFAC designations targeting Mayos-connected entities
- Rocha case — whether Chapitos-linked or Mayos-linked officials are targeted

## Key Distinction
The Mayos were historically the more powerful and better-organized half of the Sinaloa Cartel. El Mayo's capture was a sting/kidnapping by Joaquín Guzmán López, not a military defeat. The faction retains significant operational capacity, but leadership succession uncertainty limits its options.

# Actor: Cártel del Noreste (CDN)

**Role:** Cartel — Northeast Mexico border corridor
**Current Leaders:** Abdón Federico Rodríguez García ("El Cucho", #2), Francisco Daniel Esqueda ("Franky Esqueda", tactical ops Nuevo Laredo). Previous leader González arrested 2025.
**Status:** Active — mature border monopoly
**Themes:** cartel_security, us_mexico

## Who
CDN emerged from the post-Zetas schism in Tamaulipas. Controls the Nuevo Laredo-Reynosa-Matamoros border corridor. Primary revenue: migrant smuggling (migration surges under Trump), border tolls/extortion, KFR, cocaine transit. Secondary: fuel theft from Pemex pipelines.

Unlike CJNG (expansionist) or Sinaloa factions (succession-war), CDN is a mature criminal franchise operating a stable geographical monopoly. Violence in Tamaulipas is lower than 2010-2014 peak but structurally embedded. US and Mexican enforcement focus is elsewhere (CJNG, Sinaloa), giving CDN space.

## Key vulnerabilities
- Revenue dependent on US border policy — migration volumes drive income
- US FTO designation opens door to sanctions and potential kinetic action
- Competition from Zetas Vieja Escuela (aligned with Gulf Cartel)
- Tamaulipas state penetration is generational — harder to dislodge than measure

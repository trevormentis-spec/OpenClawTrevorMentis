# Actor: CJNG — Cártel Jalisco Nueva Generación (Post-Mencho)

**Former Leader:** Nemesio Oseguera Cervantes ("El Mencho") — killed in operation Feb 22, 2026
**Current Leader:** Juan Carlos Valencia González ("El Pelón" / "03" / "R-3") — confirmed Apr 2026
**Status:** Active — post-founder leadership transition underway
**Themes:** cartel_security

## Who
Mexico's most expansionary and violent cartel. Founded ~2009 as escisión from the Zetas-CNT in Michoacán. Under El Mencho's 15-year leadership, grew from a regional Michoacán gang to Mexico's dominant criminal organization with presence in 40+ countries.

## Key Event: El Mencho Killed (Feb 22, 2026)
- Killed by Mexican Army special forces in Tapalpa, Jalisco
- Operation led by Omar García Harfuch personally
- Toll: 25 National Guard, 1 bodyguard, 1 civilian killed — 30 cartel gunmen killed
- Body positively identified at morning briefing Feb 23
- This was the most significant cartel leader kill since the Mexican state killed Beltrán Leyva (2009)

## Current Leadership: "El Pelón"
- Juan Carlos Valencia González — El Mencho's stepson
- Born in California (US citizenship) — complicates extradition
- Previously led CJNG elite armed units "Élite" and "Delta"
- Known as "03", "R-3", "JP"
- $5 million US reward
- Confirmed new leader by WSJ (March 18, 2026) and El País (April 6, 2026)
- Key question: can he hold the organization together without El Mencho's personal authority?

## Leadership Succession Context
- **Menchito** (Rubén Oseguera González, El Mencho's son): sentenced to life in US prison — cannot succeed
- **Antonio Oseguera** (El Mencho's brother): extradited to US
- **Erick Valencia** ("El 85"): co-founder of CJNG, extradited to US, cooperating with DOJ
- **Abigael González** ("El Cuini"): former senior CJNG leader, cooperating with US justice
- The traditional family succession line is destroyed. El Pelón is a stepson and military commander, not the natural dynastic heir.

## What They Want
- Maintain CJNG territorial dominance (currently 40+ states/countries)
- Prevent internal fracturing as post-Mencho factions emerge
- Protect fentanyl and meth production infrastructure from heightened post-Mencho attention
- Neutralize Cárteles Unidos in Michoacán contestation
- Replace lost leadership personnel (El 85 and El Cuini cooperating means deep institutional knowledge loss)

## What They Control
- Methamphetamine production (largest producer in Mexico)
- Fentanyl precursor importation and production
- Pacific coast smuggling routes (Manzanillo, Lázaro Cárdenas ports)
- Territory across 20+ Mexican states
- Jalisco state government relationship (deepest state penetration of any cartel)
- Distribution networks in 40+ countries (Americas, Europe, Australia, Asia)
- Gas station network for money laundering and fuel theft

## What They Fear
- Further leadership decapitation by Mexican military/US intelligence
- Internal succession conflict — El Pelón's authority is untested and inherited, not earned
- CJNG heavyweights cooperating with US providing evidence against the organization
- Loss of Jalisco state protection — the deepest institutional capture relationship in Mexico
- Cárteles Unidos counter-offensive exploiting CJNG transition weakness

## Observable Indicators
- El Pelón confirmed sightings/communications — leadership consolidation signal
- CJNG internal violence — leadership challenge indicator
- Michoacán territorial battle intensity — CU exploiting CJNG transition
- Cárteles Unidos messaging/public positioning — increased confidence would signal CJNG weakness
- DEA/DOJ cooperation announcements from El 85/Cuini — would expose CJNG operational methodology
- Fentanyl/meth seizure volumes in CJNG-controlled corridors — organizational integrity proxy

# Actor: Marcelo Ebrard Casaubón

**Role:** Secretary of Economy, Mexico
**Status:** Active — Sheinbaum cabinet, appointed June 2024
**Themes:** economy_markets, us_mexico
**Born:** October 10, 1959, Mexico City (age 66)

## Who
- Former Foreign Secretary (2018-2023) under AMLO — longest-serving Mexican foreign secretary in decades
- Sheinbaum's main rival for the 2024 Morena presidential nomination
- Placed as Economy Secretary as a consolidation move — brings the Ebrard faction into the cabinet
- Former Head of Government of Mexico City (2006-2012)
- Multilateral diplomacy specialist — ran Mexico's foreign policy through Trump's first term, COVID, and early US-China trade war

## What They Want
- Successfully manage the USMCA 2026 Joint Review (July 1 start) — this is his defining portfolio objective
- Attract FDI for nearshoring — his public framing is "Mexico's moment" for supply chain relocation
- Reform Mexico's investment climate to capture the Chinese decoupling opportunity
- Maintain positive markets relationship (peso approval on his appointment)
- Rebuild political capital after losing the 2024 nomination — economy portfolio as 2030 pathway

## What They Control
- Ministry of Economy: trade policy, industry promotion, FDI attraction, USMCA implementation, tariffs
- US-Mexico trade relationship — lead negotiator with USTR Jamieson Greer
- International investment promotion apparatus
- Relations with US business community and Mexican private sector
- A faction within Morena from his 2024 presidential campaign

## What They Fear
- The probe over his son living at a diplomatic residence in the UK (opened April 2026) escalating into a corruption scandal
- USMCA review producing punitive outcomes that damage Mexico's economy on his watch
- Being outflanked by AMLO loyalists who oppose his market-friendly economic approach (tariffs on China were partially his initiative)
- Becoming irrelevant if Sheinbaum consolidates power without needing his faction

## Observable Indicators
- USMCA review meeting frequency and tone with USTR — more meetings = negotiation progressing
- Tariff actions against China — the 1,500-product tariff proposal is his signature trade policy move
- FDI quarterly data — his primary performance metric
- Son's residence probe status — if charges filed, his political position collapses
- Mexican Business Coordinating Council (CCE) tone toward his ministry — business confidence signal
- Public distance from Sheinbaum on trade policy — factional tension indicator

## Watch Items
- USMCA Joint Review (July 1, 2026) — defining moment for his tenure
- Whether Sheinbaum gives him public cover on the residence probe
- Nearshoring FDI announcements — claimed wins vs actual investment

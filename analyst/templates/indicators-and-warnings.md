# Indicators & Warnings — {Topic}

I&W is the discipline of committing, **before the fact**, to what would change
your mind. If you can't fill this in, you don't have a judgment — you have a
hunch.

---

## Baseline

> _One sentence: what does "no significant change" look like?_

---

## Indicators

Each indicator is a concrete, observable, time-bounded event.

| #   | Indicator                                          | Direction       | Watching where             | Lead time |
|-----|----------------------------------------------------|-----------------|----------------------------|-----------|
| I1  | _Specific observable event_                        | Confirms H1     | _source / channel_         | _hours/days_ |
| I2  | _…_                                                | Disconfirms H1  | _…_                        | _…_       |
| I3  | _…_                                                | Confirms H2     | _…_                        | _…_       |
| I4  | _…_                                                | Wildcard tell   | _…_                        | _…_       |

---

## Decision rules

- ≥ 2 confirming indicators of H1 in {window} → tighten H1 band.
- 1 disconfirming indicator of H1 → re-run ACH (don't quietly downgrade).
- Any wildcard tell → stand up an ad-hoc assessment within {window}.

---

## Anti-patterns

- "X tensions rise" is not an indicator. It's a vibe. Indicators are events.
- Indicators that are guaranteed to fire ("there will be a statement from
  X") are decorative. The bar is "moves the band".
- Indicators only on the "confirming" side. You need disconfirmers too —
  otherwise you're confirming-bias instrumentation.

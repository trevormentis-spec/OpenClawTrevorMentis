# Creative Angles — Week 1 (2026-05-15)

### 1. Cartel FPV Drone Adoption
**Hypothesis:** Cartels are adopting FPV drone tactics (surveillance, IED delivery, command-detonation) from Ukraine/Russia conflict playbooks. 
**Evidence:** CU uses IEDs against military patrols in Michoacán. FPV drones are cheap ($500-$2000), commercially available, and effective. Multiple Mexican states have reported drone overflights near military installations.
**Scoring:** N:5, CV:4 (subscriber-relevant — new threat vector), EB:2 (anecdotal), TTE:2 (next 3-6 months will produce evidence). **Total: 13**

### 2. CFE Substation Siting × Cartel Territory Correlation
**Hypothesis:** CFE electrical substation locations correlate with cartel territorial control maps — either because cartels target energy infrastructure where they operate, or because CFE builds less in contested areas.
**Evidence:** No systematic analysis exists. Anecdotally, Guanajuato substation density in SRLC territory is high (energy theft infrastructure). 
**Scoring:** N:4, CV:3 (energy investors, data center operators), EB:1 (speculative), TTE:3 (achievable with INEGI map overlay). **Total: 11**

### 3. AMLO-Faction vs Sheinbaum-Faction Morena State Organizations
**Hypothesis:** A systematic content analysis of Morena state-level organization leadership (who controls state party apparatus) can reveal which faction is winning the internal battle.
**Evidence:** The Rocha case split is visible at national level. Applying faction identification to all 32 states would show the organizational balance of power.
**Scoring:** N:4, CV:4 (political risk investors, US policy makers), EB:2 (Rocha case + party chief selection known), TTE:4 (achievable with 2-3 days of systematic Spanish-language reading). **Total: 13**

### 4. Fentanyl Street Prices as Lagged Seizure Indicator
**Hypothesis:** Wholesale fentanyl pricing in US cities (DEA data) with a 60-90 day lag correlates with precursor seizure volumes at Manzanillo/Lázaro Cárdenas ports.
**Evidence:** Academic literature on precursor interdiction economics supports this general relationship. Not tested for Mexico specifically.
**Scoring:** N:4, CV:5 (predictive intelligence product), EB:2 (academic support but Mexico-specific gap), TTE:3 (DEA data quarterly, port data monthly). **Total: 14**

### 5. World Cup 2026 Security — Cartel Threat Calculus Change
**Hypothesis:** Major international events are traditionally "off limits" for cartel violence (bad for business). World Cup 2026 may change this calculus due to: (a) cartels designated as FTOs, removing traditional self-interest in protecting tourists; (b) Rocha/Chapitos tension makes federal embarrassment a strategic tool.
**Evidence:** No precedent for FTO-designated cartels during a Mexico-hosted World Cup. The sovereignty dynamic is new.
**Scoring:** N:5, CV:5 (tour operators, insurers, FIFA, US State Dept), EB:1 (no precedent), TTE:1 (only 11 months until June 2027). **Total: 12**

### 6. Sheinbaum's Harfuch Succession Timing
**Hypothesis:** Sheinbaum is deliberately elevating Harfuch (El Mencho kill, high-profile mañanera appearances, media profile) to position him as her successor, which would give her a reason to remain in office through 2030 (consolidating her legacy without risking AMLO faction taking over in 2030).

**Scoring:** N:3, CV:3 (political risk), EB:3 (multiple profiles cite 2030 speculation), TTE:2 (cautious — events 4 years out). **Total: 11**

### 7. Pemex Debt × Cartel Infrastructure Targeting Correlation
**Hypothesis:** Pemex's $105B debt burden reduces pipeline anti-tapping investment, creating a predictable cartel targeting pattern where Pemex has deferred SCADA modernization.
**Evidence:** Pemex SCADA coverage at ~40-50% of critical pipelines. Maintenance investment falls during budget austerity. Pipeline taps concentrated in under-invested regions.
**Scoring:** N:4, CV:4 (energy investors, Pemex bondholders), EB:2 (indirect evidence), TTE:3 (achievable with Pemex IR data + SESNSP tap statistics). **Total: 13**

### 8. Deportation Flows × Cartel Recruitment Pipeline
**Hypothesis:** Trump-era mass deportations to northern Mexico border cities are feeding cartel recruitment — deportees with US ties, language skills, and financial desperation are ideal mid-level operators.
**Evidence:** Anectodal reporting on deportee recruitment. CDN and other border cartels would benefit directly. Not systematically studied.
**Scoring:** N:4, CV:3 (migration policy analysis), EB:1 (mostly anecdotal), TTE:2 (migration data + violence correlation achievable). **Total: 10**

### 9. Judicial Reform × Investment Climate Leading Indicator
**Hypothesis:** The passage of popular election of judges (Sheinbaum's judicial reform) will be followed by a measurable divergence in Mexico credit spreads vs peer emerging markets — the market will price the rule-of-law risk within 90 days of passage.
**Evidence:** Historical pattern: judicial independence shocks produce EM spread widening (Poland 2019, Turkey 2021, Israel 2023). Mexico's reform is structurally similar but markets have not yet priced it.
**Scoring:** N:3, CV:5 (bond traders, EM investors), EB:3 (historical analogies strong), TTE:4 (post-passage, 90-day window). **Total: 15**

### 10. Bajío Water Crisis × Data Center Investment Constraint
**Hypothesis:** Bajío water scarcity (Querétaro, Guanajuato, Aguascalientes) will become the binding constraint on data center expansion in the region, overriding CFE power availability as the primary infrastructure concern.
**Evidence:** Data center cooling is water-intensive. Bajío aquifers are declining at unsustainable rates. Municipal water restrictions are increasing. KIO MEX8 was built in CDMX, not Querétaro.
**Scoring:** N:5, CV:5 (AWS/Microsoft/KIO investors, CFE planning), EB:2 (indirect — water data + data center expansion plans known separately), TTE:3 (water restriction announcements next dry season). **Total: 15**

**Scoring:** N:5, CV:3 (political forecasting), EB:1 (Rocha is single data point), TTE:2 (elections in 2027). **Total: 11**
